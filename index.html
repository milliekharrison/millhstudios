About

Millie Harrison

I'm a maker and painter based in Edinburgh. The last few years I've worked on the tech side of a consultancy, and recently I've been spending more time on what I actually want to do, which is making things with my hands.

I'm building toward prop making and prop design for film and theatre. My work covers brass and metalwork, foam, painting, and chainmaille. I'm looking for ways in: assistant roles, volunteer work, anything where I can learn on a real production.

Section One · Prop Making

Builds

These are my first builds, made on my own as I figure out the craft. Each one was a chance to focus on something specific: shaping metal, sculpting foam, replicating a piece from reference.

01 / Brass Butterfly

Brass Butterfly

Inspired by Jinx's butterfly from Arcane. Hand-formed from brass sheet, working from screen references to get the proportions and finish right. Still a work in progress.

Metalwork · Brass · Screen Replica · Work in Progress

02 / Sidearm

Painted Sci-Fi Sidearm

A painted and weathered sci-fi pistol. Layered base coats, dry-brushing on the edges, and silver paint scraped through, so it reads as something actually used rather than a clean toy.

Paint Finish · Weathering · Prop Treatment

03 / Bar Sign

Bar Sign

A hand-built bar sign made from MDF and foam. Practice in signage fabrication, layering, and the kind of finishing work that comes up in set dressing.

Foam · MDF · Set Dressing

In Progress Images coming soon
04 / Rocky

Rocky · Project Hail Mary

A foam build of Rocky, the alien from Andy Weir's Project Hail Mary. Focused on organic sculpting, the armature underneath, and getting the texture to look believable.

Foam Fabrication · Sculpting · Replica
